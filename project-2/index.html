How To Sew A Ladder Stitch

Home · About · Contact

How often do you find that you have unravelled along the seams of your t-shirts or tears forming in your favorite sweater? Don'sweat sweat it, just sew it! The Ladder stitch, also known as the Invisible stitch, easily fixes seams without showing the thread. You'll never even know there used to be a tear!

Materials · The Process


Materials

You don't need much to get started! Some basic sewing materials is all you need.

  • Sewing Thread (color does not need to match the clothing)
  • Needle
  • Scissors
  • Clothing or fabric that needs fixing

The Process

  1. Begin by threading the needle with a double thread. Be sure to give yourself a generous amount of thread for the size of the area that needs fixing. Keep in mind that you want to double over your thread and tie the two ends together in a knot, you will not tie the thread directly to the needle or only tie a single knot on one side (Note: a double knot will be much stronger than a single knot, especially for keeping your clothing held together).


  2. Start on one side of your tear by stringing the needle through the backside or inside of your clothing. This will mean than the knot will be unseen from the outside.

  3. Bring the needle directly across to the other side and insert the needle on the outside fabric. Without fulling bringing the needle through, poke the needle back out of the fabric a little bit above your current spot and fully pull through.


  4. Continue to repeat step 3. Cross over to the other side, insert and exit, and pull through. Repeating this process will cause a ladder pattern to form along the two edges of your unravelled seams. Here is a fantastic visual of how your stitch should look:


    5. Note that only the horizontal stitches are shown on the right side of your fabric, and the vertical stitches are on the inside.

  5. Once you have completed the stitch for the length of your unravelled seams, pull the thread tight. Voila! Like magic, your seams have disappeared and your clothing is whole again!


  6. To finish your garment, simply knot your thread on the inside of your fabric and cut the thread.
